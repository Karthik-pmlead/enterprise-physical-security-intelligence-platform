# IoT Modernization Platform

## Business Problem

Many enterprises still operate legacy IoT and operational technology (OT) environments built on proprietary protocols, disconnected devices, and on-premises infrastructure. These systems are difficult to scale, secure, integrate, and analyze, limiting digital transformation initiatives.

## Modernization Strategy

Transform legacy infrastructure into a cloud-connected, event-driven IoT platform without disrupting day-to-day operations.

## Platform Capabilities

* Legacy device onboarding
* Protocol translation (Modbus, BACnet, OPC-UA, MQTT)
* Secure IoT gateways
* Edge computing for low-latency decisions
* Device digital twins
* Fleet-wide device management
* OTA firmware and configuration updates
* Cloud integration and analytics

## Modernization Architecture

**Legacy Devices → Edge Gateway → Event Streaming → Device Registry & Digital Twins → Risk & Policy Engine → Cloud Analytics → Enterprise Applications**

## Business Outcomes

* Extend the life of existing infrastructure
* Reduce migration risk and downtime
* Improve device visibility and monitoring
* Enable predictive maintenance
* Strengthen security through centralized policy management
* Accelerate cloud and AI adoption

## Shared Platform Components

This modernization use case reuses the same core platform architecture:

* Event Streaming
* Edge Processing
* Device Management
* Identity & Trust
* Risk Engine
* Policy Engine
* Observability & Analytics

Only the device protocols and migration workflows change, demonstrating how a reusable enterprise platform can modernize diverse IoT environments while preserving existing operational investments.
